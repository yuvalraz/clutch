## Quarantine ledger

Claims that are in the canon but must not drive the product. Ordered by how far
each one could bend Clutch the wrong way if left unmarked.

1. **Ego-depletion / glucose-willpower** (Barkley, *Executive Functions*, 2012).
   Barkley leans on Baumeister's "willpower is a depletable fuel pool, refill it
   with glucose and breaks." Two problems. The science is a replication-crisis
   casualty, and 2012 predates the collapse. And it's a fuel model inside a
   performance-not-drive book, so it contradicts Barkley's own framing and
   Clutch's. If the problem were a fuel tank, "fuel" is right and "transmission"
   is wrong. Hard quarantine: nothing in Clutch refills willpower.

2. **"82 studies, everything but medication failed"** (Dodson, *Defining
   Features*). Read flat, this refutes Clutch. But the evidence he cites ("the
   benefits disappeared when the structure was removed") is prosthesis-by-design
   scored as failure. Same data, opposite reading. Dodson's own practice
   (body-doubling, interest-injection) contradicts his slide. Clutch bets on
   Barkley's reading of this exact evidence. Flagged so it stays a chosen bet,
   not an unexamined one.

3. **Alpha-agonists for RSD, and "omnipotential"** (Dodson). Clinical lore, no
   RCTs behind the certainty. "Omnipotential" is a capability claim, not a
   finding. Quarantine both.

4. **Cerebellum / balance-training** (Hallowell, *ADHD 2.0*). An n=1 anecdote
   riding on the Dore program, which failed replication. Quarantine.

5. **The Wall-of-Awful neuroscience** (Mahan). "Same brain impact as trauma,"
   the amygdala/PFC mechanism he self-hedges as "where I make things cloudy,"
   the "RRE" coinage. Self-coined metaphor, filed at the same tier as
   Hallowell's race-car brain. The *model* (bricks, prevent-a-brick, the
   recognition bell) is load-bearing for the product. The *neuroscience* is not
   science. Keep the first, quarantine the second.

6. **"After 21 it's set"** (Barkley, 30 Essential Ideas). Developmental
   absolutism, overstated. Low priority, noted.
