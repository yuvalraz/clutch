# Glossary

Every private term the README and [ANNOTATIONS.md](ANNOTATIONS.md) lean on,
defined in my own words. Concepts that come from the research are attributed
to their authors; the rest are my coinages. Ordered so a stranger can read top
to bottom: the thesis first, the sources' concepts second, the design rules
and the method last.

## The thesis

### Transmission, not fuel (why "Clutch")

A clutch couples an engine to the wheels. In Barkley's account of ADHD the
engine already runs: the knowledge and the intent exist, and what fails is the
coupling between intention and action. So the product supplies coupling, on
the bet that supplying more fuel (advice, motivation content, pep) changes
nothing.

### Executive function as a performance system (Barkley)

Barkley's foundational claim: "information is not self-regulation." The
executive system is closer to a motor system than to a sensory one, so people
with ADHD fail at doing what they already know, and teaching them more has
little effect. This is why Clutch contains no advice.

### Sustain-failure

What I call the failure mode Clutch targets. Launching a project is cheap for
an interest-based nervous system; novelty carries the opening stretch, and
projects die later, when the novelty drains and the boring plumbing starts.
Most ADHD tooling instruments initiation and time-blindness. Nothing
instruments day ten of a project you no longer find interesting.

### Pricing-failure reframe

My read on abandonment, built on Brown's claim that interest is an emotion.
A dead project is an affective mismatch rather than a willpower gap: the fun
part was paid for in novelty, and the finish bills in importance, a currency
the ADHD brain can't spend. Re-pricing the finish (witnesses, publicness,
recognition) moves the wall; trying harder doesn't.

### Coast-to-collapse (Brown)

From Brown's *Smart but Stuck*: intelligence masks the executive-function
deficit. A smart person coasts on raw ability, which delays the wall for
years and makes the eventual crash more shame-laden, since "you're so smart"
was the standing explanation the whole way down. This is the profile Clutch
is built for.

### Prosthesis is permanent

The strongest convergence of the whole canon: Barkley, Brown, Hallowell, and
Dodson all say external supports for ADHD are prosthetics by design, kept for
life. Nobody graduates from glasses. Clutch accepts that up front instead of
promising you'll one day stop needing it.

### Extended phenotype (Barkley, 2012)

Barkley borrows Dawkins' term: executive function includes the capacity to
rearrange your external environment so that it regulates you. On that reading
a harness is the function itself running at software scale, the same move as
a visible calendar or a timer, and calling it a crutch misses that the
function was always meant to reach outside the skull.

## The sources' concepts

### Point of performance (Barkley)

Interventions work where and when the breakdown happens, and their effect
degrades with distance in space and time from that moment. This is the
academic argument for just-in-time intervention: a bell that fires in context
beats a dashboard reviewed later.

### Hot/cool EF split (Barkley, 2012)

Cool executive functions handle the what, where, and when: working memory,
planning, foresight. Hot ones handle the why: motivation and emotion. The hot
side is where interest lives, which makes this the circuitry under Brown's
"interest is an emotion" and Dodson's interest-based nervous system, three
independent voices on one idea.

### Ignition vs brakes (Brown)

ADHD is usually described as a braking problem, disinhibition. Brown points
at ignition: when interest dies nothing starts, and fear becomes the ignition
of last resort. Clutch's design goal is a non-fear ignition.

### "Motivation is not gas in the tank" (Brown)

Brown's line from *Smart but Stuck*, rejecting the idea of motivation as a
fuel you hold in reserve and burn down. Motivation is emotion-dependent, not
a stored quantity. A second independent voice against the fuel model, which
hardens the ego-depletion quarantine (ledger entry 1) from "Barkley
contradicts himself" into "the affective-EF school rejects fuel."

### Context-dependent motivation / amygdala-as-Google (Brown)

Brown's observation in *Smart but Stuck* that motivation shifts with where
you are, who is present, and what the moment means, not just with the task.
His amygdala-as-Google image: the brain searches emotional memory by current
context and ranks what comes back. This is why the recognition bell must
read context, not only task state.

### Possible selves (Brown)

From *Smart but Stuck*: a person carries competing, emotion-laden images of
who they might become, and the self that gets fed wins. The recognition bell
feeds the graduate-self so it beats the failure-self.

### Scaffold-dependence is legitimate (Brown)

Brown frames ongoing dependence on external structure as support, not
failure. That makes him the third independent voice on the 82-studies fork
(ledger entry 2), a vote for the reading Clutch already bet on.

### The CEO's three assistants (Brown)

A case from *Smart but Stuck*: a successful executive whose success is the
ongoing scaffold of three assistants covering what his own executive
functions don't. The standing answer to "isn't a permanent crutch a
failure?" His success never outgrew the scaffold; it is the scaffold.

### Interest-based nervous system (Dodson)

Dodson's model: the ADHD nervous system engages on interest, novelty,
challenge, urgency, and competition, and stays inert to importance, rewards,
and consequences. Importance-denominated instructions bounce off it, which is
the same observation the pricing-failure reframe prices in.

### RSD / pre-emptive surrender (Dodson)

Rejection-sensitive dysphoria: rejection and criticism land with
disproportionate pain, and one coping mode is surrendering before the attempt
so the rejection can't happen. The surrender mechanism bridges to Mahan's
bricks. Dodson's clinical certainty about alpha-agonist treatment for RSD
sits in the quarantine ledger (entry 3); the mechanism stays.

### Saltatory cognition / feel-first foresight (Dodson)

Dodson's term for ADHD thought that jumps, arriving at conclusions without
the visible intermediate steps. I apply it to what I call feel-first
foresight: the conclusion arrives as a feeling first and the argument gets
reconstructed afterward. It also names the spark-protocol premise.

### "Owner's manual" (Dodson)

Dodson's phrase: every person with ADHD needs an individual owner's manual,
because the condition presents differently in each person. It's the closest
existing name for Clutch's category.

### The bricks / Wall of Awful (Mahan)

Brendan Mahan's model of task avoidance. Every shamed abandonment lays
bricks: one for the failure, one for each person disappointed, one for the
rejection. The bricks stack into a wall in front of the next attempt at that
task, which is how quitting today raises the cost of starting tomorrow. His
neuroscience for the model is quarantined (ledger entry 5); the model is
load-bearing.

### RRE (Mahan)

Mahan's self-coined acronym, tied to his Wall of Awful model. My sources
never expand it, so I don't guess at the expansion here. It's filed with the
rest of his self-coined neuroscience in the quarantine ledger (entry 5),
while the bricks model itself stays load-bearing.

### The gift war (Barkley vs Hallowell)

Barkley describes ADHD as disinhibition and calls the gift framing gibberish;
Hallowell's race-car brain retells the same shared disinhibition wiring as a
gift with weak brakes. Same brain, opposite story. The edge map files it as
tension, not circuitry.

## The design rules

### Decay-trigger

The planned trigger, derived from Mahan's forward-movement-as-decay sensor:
it fires on zero forward movement, never on slowness. Slow is walking pace
and legal; zero is decay. Nothing is built yet; this is the commitment the
edge map already records.

### Prevent-a-brick / the recognition bell

The planned response, and the anti-nag constraint that separates Clutch from
a tracker. When the trigger fires, the bell recognizes what actually moved
and names the smallest next step. It never demands the far side of the wall,
because a nag lays a brick and the bell exists to prevent one.

## The method

### Quarantine ledger

The discipline in [ANNOTATIONS.md](ANNOTATIONS.md): claims that are in the
corpus but must not drive the product, listed with reasons. Ego-depletion and
glucose-willpower lead the ledger; a source being foundational doesn't exempt
its weak claims.

### The 82-studies fork

Dodson cites 82 studies in which every non-medication intervention "failed":
the benefits disappeared when the structure was removed. Read flat, that
refutes this project. Barkley reads the same evidence as prosthesis-by-design
scored as failure, and Clutch bets on Barkley's reading; quarantine ledger
entry 2 keeps it a chosen bet instead of an unexamined one.

### Resonance weights

Hand-assigned per-source weights (high / med / low) recording how much a
source maps onto my own condition. Curation by personal fit, deliberately
subjective and marked as such.

### Walking-pace floor

The build discipline: one source, one clean file, one commit. It defines the
streak's smallest legal move so the streak can't hit zero.

### The streak

My name for the unbroken chain of walking-pace commits. The walking-pace
floor defines its smallest legal move.

### Spark-protocol

A separate private protocol of mine that ANNOTATIONS references once, whose
premise Dodson's saltatory cognition names.

## Open risks

### Habituation

The spine injects near-identical wording every turn. The canon warns that
salience decays: what fired novelty on day one reads as wallpaper by week
three, and nothing in the pack yet measures whether an injected line still
changes behavior. Open by choice: any variation mechanism has to vary the
surface without varying the meaning, or it breaks the fixed-wording rule
(mechanical framing, not character reading). Until that design exists, the
detector is the user: if the bell starts reading as furniture, saying so is
the signal.
